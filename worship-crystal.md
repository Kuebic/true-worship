# Worship, in One Line

> **Worship is the life of a true child whose whole aim is to become one in heart with Heavenly Parent.**

That is the whole thing. Everything below is scaffolding around it.

---

**It succeeds when** — for that moment, your heart and Heavenly Parent's heart are one. Not when the room is moved. Not when the band plays flawlessly.

**God is always reading the heart, never the performance.** The thief on the cross could sing nothing, tithe nothing, serve no one — and worshipped perfectly with one line of love.

---

**Three stages, each keeping the last:**

- **Servant** — awe before a holy King. *Worthship.*
- **Grateful child** — *Abba.* Intimacy and thanks, still mostly receiving.
- **True child** — sees the Parent's grief and turns around to comfort it. Takes ownership.

**The turn that changes everything:** God is a *grieving* Parent (Gen 6:6; Hosea; the father running down the road), longing to be comforted by His own children.

---

**Four beats to oneness — you can't force it, only clear the way:**

1. **Clear the Self** — set aside ego, distraction, the wall.
2. **Attend** — make Heavenly Parent present. Speak to Him as "You."
3. **Give / Invest** — pour out your heart *before* anything comes back.
4. **Meet / Resonate** — His heart arrives because you made room for it.

---

**Ask, whenever you lead:** *Am I drawing near to my Parent, and is my heart meeting His?*
